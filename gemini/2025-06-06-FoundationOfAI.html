The AI Preparedness Gap

The rise of AI has created a seismic shift, demanding power, cooling, and space that most data centers were never designed to provide. As WWT Chief Technology Advisor Don Molaro states, "Realistically, almost no corporate data centers are prepared for these kinds of AI workloads at scale." This interactive dashboard explores the five key priorities leaders must address to bridge this gap and transform their facilities into AI-ready powerhouses.

Priority 1: Power Requirements

AI workloads are exceptionally power-intensive, creating an unprecedented hunger for energy that strains grids and budgets. Projections show a dramatic surge in electricity demand, making strategic power planning a matter of survival.

Priority 2: Cooling Solutions

High-density AI hardware generates intense heat that traditional air cooling cannot handle. This necessitates a systemic shift towards advanced liquid cooling to prevent thermal throttling, hardware failure, and costly downtime.

Priority 5: Sustainability

AI's potential is undermined by its massive energy and water footprint. Proactive sustainability, moving beyond a "take-make-dispose" model, is a core business imperative to mitigate reputational risk and meet corporate goals.

Key Mitigation Strategies:

  • Transition to 100% renewable energy sources.
  • Implement waste heat reuse for district heating.
  • Adopt circular economy principles (refurbish, recycle).
  • Utilize carbon-aware AI software to schedule workloads.
